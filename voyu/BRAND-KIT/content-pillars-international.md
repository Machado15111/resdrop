## PILLAR 1 — WHERE TO STAY IN BRAZIL

**Objective:** Own the phrase "where to stay in Brazil" in the minds of international travelers. Become the reference for accommodation curation across Brazil.

**Audience:** Cold — people who are Brazil-curious or already planning a trip.

**Emotional angle:** Confidence + Clarity. "Finally, someone who can tell me where to go."

**Content formats:**
- Single posts: one property featured per post, with a short why-it-works rationale
- Carousels: "Top [X] places to stay in [destination]" guides
- Reels: destination overview with accommodation focus
- Stories: quick picks — "If I were going to [city] this month, I'd stay here."

**Visual direction:** Wide, editorial property shots. Destination establishing shots. Consistent warm tone. No text-heavy layouts.

**Caption approach:**
```
Hook: Name the decision the traveler is trying to make.
Ex: "If you're figuring out where to stay in Rio de Janeiro, here's where to start."

Body: 2–3 sentences on what makes this the right choice. Specific.

Close: Soft invitation to engage or follow.
Ex: "Save this for your Brazil planning."
```

**CTA approach:** "Save this." / "Follow for curated Brazil stays." / "DM us your destination."

## PILLAR 2 — THE PROPERTIES

**Objective:** Make specific hotels and villas desirable objects. Sell the stay before the price is ever discussed.

**Audience:** Warm — followers and website visitors who are already considering Brazil.

**Emotional angle:** Desire + Aspiration. "I want to stay exactly there."

**Sub-categories:**

**2A — Hotel showcase:**
The full portrait of a single hotel. What it is, what makes it right, why Voyu recommends it.

**2B — Villa showcase:**
The full portrait of a single villa or beach house. Focus on space, privacy, and the life it enables.

**2C — Hidden gems:**
"This isn't the most famous hotel in [destination]. It's the most interesting." Anti-obvious curation.

**2D — The comparison:**
"Hotel vs Villa in [destination] — here's how we'd choose for different travelers."

**Content formats:**
- Carousels: property tour (exterior → key room → pool/view → detail)
- Single post: the one definitive shot + authoritative caption
- Reels: property walkthrough or transformation (before guests arrive → after)
- Stories: "Have you heard of [property]?" engagement opener

**Visual direction:** Beautiful, quiet, architectural. Property details > amenity lists.

**Caption approach:**
```
Open: Set the scene in 1 sentence. Don't name the hotel yet.
Ex: "There's a hotel in Santa Teresa that sits on a hillside looking down at Rio — and has a pool that catches the city light at exactly the right angle."

Develop: Name it. Say what makes it special specifically. 2–3 sentences.

Close: "This is one of the properties in our Rio collection." + CTA.
```

**CTA approach:** "See our full [destination] collection → link in bio." / "Thinking about [destination]? We know exactly where to put you."

## PILLAR 3 — DESTINATION THROUGH A STAY

**Objective:** Teach international travelers about Brazilian destinations — but always filtered through the lens of accommodation. We don't do generic guides. We do accommodation-led guides.

**Audience:** Mid-funnel — actively researching Brazil.

**Emotional angle:** Confidence + Curiosity. "I'm learning something I couldn't find on TripAdvisor."

**Content formats:**
- Carousels: "A local's guide to [neighborhood/city] — told through its best places to stay"
- Reels: "The best [X] neighborhoods to stay in [city] — and why"
- Single post: "[Destination] has many faces. Here's the one most visitors miss — and where to stay to find it."

**Destination areas to cover:**
- Rio neighborhoods: Ipanema, Leblon, Santa Teresa, Botafogo, Lapa, Barra
- Bahia coast breakdown: Trancoso vs Arraial d'Ajuda vs Caraíva vs Itacaré
- São Paulo for design travelers
- Fernando de Noronha logistics + accommodation guide
- Florianópolis: which beach, which stay
- Maragogi / Alagoas coast emerging scene

**Caption approach:**
```
Hook: Ask the question the traveler is asking.
Ex: "Trancoso or Arraial d'Ajuda? If you're going to Bahia's south coast, you need to understand the difference — because it changes where you stay."

Body: Explain the difference in 3–4 sentences. Specific, confident, not Wikipedia-style.

Close: "Here's where Voyu puts different types of travelers in each." + CTA.
```

## PILLAR 4 — BRAZIL REFRAMED

**Objective:** Address the main barrier to Brazil for international travelers: perception. Reframe Brazil as a sophisticated, beautiful, well-designed destination that rivals anywhere in the world.

**Audience:** Cold — people who haven't seriously considered Brazil yet. People comparing it to Tulum, Ibiza, Amalfi, St. Barths.

**Emotional angle:** Revelation + Desire. "I hadn't thought of Brazil that way. Now I want to go."

**Content formats:**
- Single posts: "Have you considered Brazil instead?" comparison posts
- Carousels: "Why [Brazil destination] belongs on the same list as [international equivalent]"
- Reels: Destination showcase with subtle comparison hook
- Stories: Poll — "Which would you choose for your next beach trip?" [Ibiza or Brazil coast]

**Comparison angles:**
- Trancoso vs Tulum: more authentic, less commercial, private villas
- Fernando de Noronha vs Maldives: same water clarity, wilder, more exclusive
- Bahia coast vs Amalfi: warmer, less crowded, genuinely tropical
- Maragogi vs Greek Islands: turquoise water, less traveled, emerging luxury scene
- Rio vs Barcelona: energy, food, beach — but with a skyline you can't replicate

**Safety note:** Handle the "is Brazil safe?" topic with confidence:
"Brazil has places just like any destination — and properties like these that make the experience seamless, beautiful, and completely without concern. The right stay makes all the difference."

**Caption approach:**
```
Hook: Name the comparison directly.
"You've thought about Tulum. Have you thought about Trancoso?"

Body: Draw the comparison with specifics. Be honest (Brazil is warmer, less crowded, more authentic, sometimes more affordable). Never disparage the competitor destination.

Close: "This is what Brazil looks like when you know where to stay." + CTA.
```

## PILLAR 6 — THE GUEST EXPERIENCE

**Objective:** Social proof. Show real outcomes. Let past travelers tell the story.

**Audience:** Mid-funnel — warm prospects who are close to deciding.

**Emotional angle:** Confirmation + Relief. "Other people like me did this and loved it."

**Content formats:**
- Quote creatives: real testimonials on dark background
- Story sequences: "We planned this trip — here's how it went" (with photos if available)
- Carousels: "A Voyu stay in [destination] — from inquiry to arrival"
- Before/after: "The property we booked — vs what they thought they'd get from booking alone"

**How to present testimonials:**
- Use first name + city/country of origin
- Include the destination and property (or general area)
- Include a timing reference: "[month/year]"
- If possible: include a photo from the property taken by the guest

**Sample testimonial format:**
> "We were going to book through Booking.com. A friend suggested Voyu. We couldn't be more grateful — every detail of our time in Trancoso was exactly right."
> — Sarah M., New York · Trancoso, Bahia · March 2025

## PILLAR 7 — LIFESTYLE & DESIRE

**Objective:** Emotional connection. This is pure aspiration — not selling a property, selling the feeling of being in Brazil, staying well.

**Audience:** All audiences. Especially cold.

**Emotional angle:** Pure desire. No call to action needed sometimes — just "make them want this life."

**Content formats:**
- Single posts: lifestyle moments (pool, sunset, breakfast, hammock)
- Reels: atmosphere-led (no narration, just beauty)
- Stories: "This is what a Wednesday in Trancoso can look like."

**Caption approach:**
Short. Sometimes just a location tag and one sentence. Sometimes nothing — let the image speak.

```
Examples:
"Mornings like this are why people keep coming back to Fernando de Noronha."

"Trancoso, early evening. Worth every hour of the flight."

"The pool. The view. The silence. Brazil."
```

**No CTA needed** on pure lifestyle posts. Or the softest possible: "Follow for more." The desire build is the purpose.
