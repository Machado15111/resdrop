## QUICK REFERENCE — DECISION TREE

**"Which format should I use?"**

```
Is it a single beautiful image?
  → Single feed post

Does it have 3+ things to explain or compare?
  → Carousel

Is it visual, atmospheric, motion-based?
  → Reel

Is it a quick tip, behind-the-scenes, or engagement?
  → Story sequence

Do I want to reach new audiences with paid budget?
  → Static Ad or Video Ad (pick framework 1–7)

Is it for people who already know Voyu?
  → Warm audience remarketing (framework 5–7)
```

**"Which CTA tier?"**

```
Cold audience (doesn't know Voyu yet):
→ Tier 1: "Explore where to stay in Brazil →" / "Follow for curated stays"

Warm audience (follows, has engaged):
→ Tier 2: "DM us your destination" / "Tell us where you're going"

Hot audience (has inquired or visited site):
→ Tier 3: "Plan your stay →" / "Ready to book? Link in bio."
```

## HOW TO WRITE COPY FOR A HOTEL (Step-by-step)

**Step 1 — Identify the angle for US/EU audience:**
What is the most surprising or compelling thing about this hotel for someone who knows European or US luxury travel?

**Step 2 — Find the specific detail:**
One thing that isn't in the hotel's own marketing copy. Something you know because you've been there, because someone told you, because you researched deeply.

**Step 3 — Write the hook:**
The first 1–2 sentences. Must make the reader stop. Evocative, specific, image-creating.

**Step 4 — Develop with 3 sentences:**
What the property is. What makes it right. Who it's for (without making it exclusive — aspirational but welcoming).

**Step 5 — Close with invitation:**
Not a push. An opening. "This is one of our [Destination] picks." / "DM us to learn more."

## HOW TO WRITE COPY FOR A VILLA

**The difference from a hotel:**
A villa is sold on **what life feels like inside it**, not on what amenities it has. Lead with space, privacy, and freedom — not bedrooms and square meters.

**Villa copy framework:**
```
Sentence 1: Set the scene — where is it, what does it look like from outside?
Sentence 2: What you do/feel inside it — the life it enables.
Sentence 3: The specific detail that makes this villa different from any other.
Sentence 4: Who this is for (profile without name).
Close: Invitation.
```

## BRAZIL POSITIONING CHEATSHEET

| If client is coming from... | Lead with... |
|---------------------------|-------------|
| US (NYC/LA) | Ease + beauty + discovery. "Brazil is closer than you think, and better than you've been told." |
| UK/Europe | Design + culture + authenticity. "The part of Brazil that feels genuinely different." |
| Tulum travelers | Trancoso comparison. More authentic, less commercialized, private villas. |
| Ibiza/Med travelers | Bahia coast comparison. Warmer water, fewer crowds, extraordinary food. |
| Maldives/Bali travelers | Fernando de Noronha or Maragogi. Same water quality, far more character. |
| Caribbean travelers | Bahia coast. Better food, more design, similar (or better) water. |

## VOYU BRAND CHECKLIST — Before Publishing Anything

```
□ Does this feel like it belongs in a premium travel magazine?
□ Is there one clear message (not two competing ones)?
□ Is the logo placed correctly and legibly?
□ Does the copy use specific details (not generic adjectives)?
□ Is there no mention of price, discount, or "best deal"?
□ Does the CTA match the audience temperature?
□ Is the image warm-toned and editorial?
□ Does the typography follow the brand kit (Cormorant + DM Sans)?
□ Would a traveler who reads Condé Nast Traveler trust this brand?
□ Does this make Brazil look desirable, sophisticated, and worth it?
```

If any answer is "no" — revise before publishing.
