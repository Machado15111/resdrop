## PART 1 — LOGO ANALYSIS & VISUAL DNA

### What the logo communicates (decoded):

**The icon** is a masterpiece of dual symbolism:
- **Outer shape:** An eye (almond/petal form) — *"we see"* — curation, discernment, vision
- **Inner element:** A location pin (the pointed base) — *"we find"* — discovery, destination, place
- **Concentric circles:** Like a camera iris, like ripples in water, like the aperture of a watchful eye — *precision, depth, focus*

**Combined reading:** "The eye that finds your place." A brand that sees more than you can — and delivers it. This is the perfect icon for a curated accommodation specialist. It's geometric, modern, travel-specific, and premium.

**The wordmark:** "VOYU TRAVEL" in wide-set capitals, thin weight — communicates:
- Space and confidence (not rushing)
- Editorial sensibility (like a magazine masthead)
- International modernity (no regional clichés)
- The kind of refinement associated with luxury hospitality brands

### The logo's inherent brand personality:
The logo does not scream. It does not perform. It simply exists with quiet authority — the visual equivalent of a property that doesn't need to advertise because its reputation precedes it.

**This is the aesthetic law the entire brand must follow.**

## PART 3 — TYPOGRAPHY SYSTEM

### Recommended Type Stack:

**Display / Headlines — Serif:**
> `Cormorant Garamond` (Google Fonts — free) or `Playfair Display`
> Weight: Light (300) for large headlines, Regular (400) for subheads
> Character: Editorial, sophisticated, timeless. The typeface luxury hospitality brands use.
> Use for: Hero headlines, property names, destination titles

**Body / Interface — Geometric Sans:**
> `DM Sans` or `Inter` or `Montserrat Light`
> Weight: Light (300) for captions, Regular (400) for body, Medium (500) for labels
> Character: Modern, clean, international, readable at small sizes
> Use for: Captions, descriptions, CTAs, UI text, all non-headline copy

**Wordmark Reference:**
The VOYU TRAVEL wordmark appears to use a geometric, wide-tracked capital sans. For brand consistency, use this style for subheadings that accompany the logo — all-caps, generous letter-spacing (tracking: +200 in design tools).

### Typography Rules:

**Headlines (Cormorant Garamond):**
- Large, airy, confident — let the type breathe
- Avoid bold weight — thin and refined always
- Mix roman and italic for visual interest in carousels
- Line height: 1.1–1.2 for display, 1.4 for body

**Captions & Body (DM Sans):**
- Light or Regular only — Medium for labels/CTAs
- Wider tracking for short all-caps labels (+100 to +150)
- No tight tracking on body text

**What to avoid:**
- Script fonts — too romantic, too wedding-adjacent
- Heavy/Black weights — goes against the logo's line weight
- More than 2 typefaces in one creative piece
- Decorative or novelty fonts of any kind

## PART 4 — LOGO USAGE RULES

### The 4 Logo Versions:

**Version A — Primary (Logo on Dark)**
- Icon (Areia) + Wordmark (Areia) on Noite background
- **Default version.** Use on all dark backgrounds, dark photography, brand-primary creatives.

**Version B — Reversed (Logo on Light)**
- Icon (Noite) + Wordmark (Noite) on Branco Quente or Creme
- Use on light editorial pieces, light ad variants, documents.

**Version C — Icon Only**
- The eye-pin icon alone, in Areia
- Use when full wordmark is too large for the space (e.g., circular profile crops, small watermarks on images, story corner placement)

**Version D — Wordmark Only**
- "VOYU TRAVEL" text only, in Areia
- Use for horizontal layouts where the icon would compete with imagery

### Placement Rules:

| Context | Preferred Position | Size |
|---------|-------------------|------|
| Feed post | Bottom-center or bottom-right | 12–18% of frame width |
| Story | Top-left or bottom-center | 15–20% of frame width |
| Carousel (first slide) | Center or bottom-center | 20–28% of frame width |
| Ad creative | Bottom-left or bottom-right | 10–15% of frame width |
| Reel cover | Bottom-center or top-left | 15% of frame width |
| Email header | Centered | Full header width / max 200px |

### Logo Safe Space:
- Minimum clear space around logo: equal to the height of the letter "V" in VOYU on all sides
- Never crowd the logo with other elements, text, or competing imagery

### Logo Don'ts:
```
❌ Never stretch or distort the logo
❌ Never change the logo colors outside the approved versions
❌ Never place the logo on busy, low-contrast imagery without overlay
❌ Never add drop shadows, glows, or effects to the logo
❌ Never rotate the logo
❌ Never place the logo on a colored background outside the brand palette
❌ Never use the logo smaller than 80px wide in digital contexts
```

### Logo on Photography:
- When placing the logo on a photograph, ensure there is adequate contrast
- Use a subtle dark gradient overlay (15–25% opacity dark gradient) at bottom or corner if needed
- The logo should always be legible — if it isn't, add the gradient

## PART 5 — IMAGE DIRECTION

### The Voyu Visual World:

Voyu images should feel like they belong in a high-end hospitality magazine — think *Condé Nast Traveler*, *Monocle*, *Kinfolk Travel*, *Cereal Magazine*.

**What to look for in photography:**

✅ **Light quality:** Warm, golden, natural. Sunrise/sunset > midday harsh light.
✅ **Composition:** Architectural, spacious, human scale. Wide shots with negative space.
✅ **Color temperature:** Warm, not cold. The camera should feel like it captured warmth.
✅ **Geometry:** Lines, symmetry, form — pools, facades, corridors, views.
✅ **Human presence:** Optional but powerful when done right. Silhouettes, people from behind, non-posed moments.
✅ **Details that tell a story:** The wine glass at the pool edge. The hammock facing the water. The breakfast tray on the terrace.
✅ **Brazil's natural palette:** Turquoise water, green jungle, terracotta tiles, white walls, dark wood, stone.

**What to avoid:**

❌ Overly posed, forced smiling stock photography
❌ Flat lighting (overcast, flash, midday sun without character)
❌ Busy, cluttered compositions
❌ Clichéd tropical imagery (clip-art parrots, tourist-brochure beaches)
❌ Heavy Instagram filters or oversaturated edits
❌ Cold, blue-toned editing (goes against the brand's warmth)

### Image Treatment/Editing Direction:

**For brand consistency, apply this editing logic:**
- Warm the highlights slightly (+5 to +10 warmth)
- Lift shadows gently (no crushed blacks — retain detail)
- Increase clarity slightly for architectural details
- Reduce clarity for portraits and soft moments
- Subtle vignette acceptable on dark-themed creatives
- Color grading: lean warm amber/sand in highlights, cool sage in shadows (split-tone gives editorial quality)

## PART 6 — LAYOUT SYSTEM

### Grid Rules:

**For Feed Posts (1:1 and 4:5):**
- Primary zone (center 60%): main imagery
- Safety zone (outer 15%): keep logo, text, and critical elements inside
- Text placement: Bottom third preferred, or centered with generous negative space
- Text area: Max 40% of the frame (the image speaks first)

**For Stories (9:16):**
- Top 20%: Logo or destination label
- Middle 60%: Primary image or video
- Bottom 20%: CTA or caption text
- Or: Full-bleed image with text overlay at top and bottom

**For Carousels:**
- Slide 1: Brand cover — image + logo + minimal headline
- Slides 2–N: Content with consistent typography style
- Final slide: CTA + logo + contact/website
- Consistent typography position across slides (same top margin, same bottom margin)

**For Ad Creatives (1:1 for feed, 9:16 for stories):**
- 80% image, 20% text (in creative itself)
- Logo always present, always legible
- One clear headline — never two competing messages
- One CTA — never multiple options

### Spacing Principles:
- Generous is always better than crowded
- When in doubt, remove one element
- Margins: minimum 5% of frame width on all edges
- The logo needs its own breathing room — it should never touch other elements

## PART 7 — ICON & GRAPHIC ELEMENTS

### Supporting Graphic Language (derived from the logo):

**The concentric circle motif:**
The circles inside the logo can be used as a subtle graphic device:
- As a watermark/background texture (very faint, 5–10% opacity)
- As a framing device around a location name
- As a "stamp" element on brand collateral

**Line weight:**
The logo's thin line weight sets the standard. All graphic elements should use thin lines:
- Dividers: 0.5–1px
- Borders/frames: 0.5–1px
- Icons: outlined, thin stroke (not filled)
- Never thick borders or heavy graphic frames

**Recommended icon set:**
Use an outlined, thin-stroke icon library for any UI elements or pictograms. Options:
- Feather Icons
- Phosphor Icons (Regular weight)
- Heroicons (Outline)

## PART 8 — BRAND IN MOTION (REELS & VIDEO)

### Video Aesthetic Direction:

**Pacing:** Slow, deliberate — cinematic, not frenetic. Let shots breathe.

**Shot selection for Reels:**
- Wide establishing shots of the property (3–5 seconds)
- Detail shots: water surface, architecture, textures (2–3 seconds each)
- Transition moments: walking into a room, sitting down, looking at a view
- Always end on a static wide or scenic shot — let the destination settle

**Text in reels:**
- Simple, minimal text overlay — one phrase at a time
- Fade in / fade out transitions — never instant text cuts
- Cormorant Garamond for titles, DM Sans for labels
- Color: Areia (#CBC3BA) on darker footage, Noite (#0A0A0A) on light footage

**Music direction:**
- Instrumental: soft jazz, bossa nova instrumental, lo-fi orchestral, ambient
- No generic "travel vlog" energetic music
- Volume: background, not dominant
- Should feel like you're already relaxed in the destination

**Reel cover design:**
- Static image from the reel (best frame, usually wide establishing shot)
- Small logo in corner (Version C — icon only)
- Minimal text label or no text (let the image speak)
- Consistent square crop for the feed grid

## PART 9 — BRAND APPLICATION RULES SUMMARY

### The 10 Laws of Voyu Creative:

1. **Warmth before cold.** Every creative should feel inviting, not corporate.
2. **Image before text.** The photo/video is the hero. Text is the voice.
3. **One message per creative.** If you're saying two things, say nothing.
4. **The logo is a mark of quality, not an advertisement.** Place it with purpose.
5. **Negative space is luxury.** Don't fill it. Let it breathe.
6. **Specific beats generic.** "The pool at UXUA facing the Quadrado" > "luxury pool in Brazil."
7. **Desire before CTA.** Make them want it first, then invite them to act.
8. **Never compete on price.** Compete on curation, beauty, confidence.
9. **Consistent type stack, always.** Cormorant + DM Sans. Nothing else.
10. **When in doubt, remove.** Less is always more for this brand.
